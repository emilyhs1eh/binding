Colophon

Emily Hsieh / 2023

In my exploration of project one design with Michael Rock's "Hooked on a Feeling," the primary focus was on delineating the substantial impact of emotions in design. Transitioning towards a more analytical perspective, I subsequently delved into Alexandra Lange's "Criticism = Love." This article intricately examines the concept of expressing admiration for design through constructive criticism. Conclusively, I directed my attention to Kristen Coogan's "Knowing Your Design History is Crucial to Aesthetic Innovation", where the discourse centers on the profound influence of historical contexts on design innovation.

What unifies these diverse perspectives is the deliberate pursuit of design harmony. Each article contributes a distinctive design element—emotions, critique, and innovation—forming an integrated narrative. My selection aims to underscore the nuanced interplay of these facets, creating a harmonious synergy that enriches the overarching theme of design.

Under the theme of "Harmony", the web design decision ties the thematic threads together. Drawing inspiration from color theory—where red, yellow, and blue harmonize into a complete palette—each color and symbol represent a facet of the design process. In addition, the sharp angles of "✸" embody the critical perspective, the soft curves of "❋" encapsulate the emotional realm, and the intricate lines of "✺" signify the innovative spirit. In this harmonious blend of colors and symbols, "Design Harmony" is visualized.

Overall, the ensemble of articles converges to create a symphony of design wisdom, where Critique, Emotion, and Innovation harmoniously dance together. This harmonious approach seeks to inspire a design philosophy that transcends mere aesthetics, embracing the holistic interplay of elements for a truly harmonized design experience.
